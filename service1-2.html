The Core Three-Day Experience

In a core three-day experience, Dayna actively works with clients on the memories and conditioning that have caused their suffering. Using this modality, which combines shamanic journey work with spiritual and therapeutic counseling, optimizes the healing of depression, anxiety, PTSD, addictions, phobias, OCD, fear of dying for terminal patients, as well as plateauing on the awakening path.

Preparatory Counseling

A preparatory counseling session of 90 - 120 minutes the day before the journey. The two initial sessions allow Dayna to understand the origins of the client's mental, emotional and physical suffering and establish therapeutic safety and connection.

Shamanic Journey

A full day shamanic journey in which Dayna actively counsels the client based on the material that has arisen in the previous sessions. This lasts approximately six hours, from 10am to 4pm.

The intention of this work is to heal and clear blocks in your consciousness, including trauma, past life material, ancestral inheritances, unconscious beliefs, misinterpretations etc. It is Dayna's job as facilitator to help you see these and meet them with love and wisdom. She does so using both a cultivated intuitive faculty as well as a great deal of experience. Sometimes what is called for is to facilitate from a place of gentle love and compassion. Other times, what may be called for is a stronger, more direct facilitation style in service to cutting through and challenging the ego constructs that are keeping you blocked. However, at all times, she will always be holding you in unconditional positive regard.

Integration Day

A full day of integration (10am to 4pm) the day after the journey. This consists of guided meditation, breathwork, chanting (if desired), somatic practices like gentle yoga, going over her notes from the day before, how to anchor the learnings and next steps in the client's life, as well as integration counseling.

Journey Documentation

Dayna also types up the journey notes and sends them to the client.

  • Depending on the level of trauma or issues being faced, Dayna will often require counseling leading up to this three-day experience. This establishes important safety and connection and helps Dayna understand and begin working with long-standing core wounds and beliefs.
